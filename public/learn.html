The Art of Coffee

From the bean to your cup, understand the journey of flavor, aroma, and craft.

Know Your Beans

Arabica: Grown at high altitudes, these beans are known for their smooth, complex flavor profiles with notes of fruit, berries, and sugar.

Robusta: Stronger and more bitter, Robusta beans have higher caffeine content and are often used in espresso blends for a rich crema.

The Roast Spectrum

Light Roast: Light brown color, no oil on surface. Highest acidity and retains most of the origin bean flavors.

Medium Roast: Medium brown, balanced flavor, aroma, and acidity. The "American favorite".

Dark Roast: Dark brown/black, oily surface. Low acidity, heavy body, and tends to reveal roasted, bitter flavors.

Brewing Methods

Master the technique that suits your taste.

Pour Over

Produces a clean, intricate cup. Best for highlighting the unique flavors of single-origin beans.

French Press

Full-bodied and rich. The immersion method retains natural oils for a robust flavor.

Espresso

Concentrated and intense. The foundation for lattes, cappuccinos, and flat whites.
